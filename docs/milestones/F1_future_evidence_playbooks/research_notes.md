# Research Notes

- The current reopen protocol already names the three lane classes, but later
  work still benefits from more concrete lane-specific routing, bundle, and
  stop-condition guidance.
- The frozen paper scope is narrow enough that later reopen work is safest when
  each lane begins from the smallest existing artifact bundle instead of
  defaulting to a fresh exploratory stage.
- Precision, systems, and compiled-boundary repairs fail in different ways, so
  they should not share one generic success criterion.
- The main scientific risk at this stage is not missing creativity but silent
  widening; these playbooks therefore emphasize hard limits and refreeze
  discipline.
