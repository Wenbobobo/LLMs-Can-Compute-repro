# E1b Systems Patch Design

## Trigger

The current `R2` gate correctly stays mixed, but it still lacks one compact
bundle that explains the mixed result in the terms the scientific claim needs:

- geometry asymptotics are strongly positive;
- current-scope `D0` end-to-end runtime is still not competitive;
- the gap must be decomposed into same-scope component costs and linked back to
  the actual history regimes seen on the positive `D0` suites.

## Goal

Add one bounded systems bundle that attributes current-scope cost, bridges the
geometry benchmark to the present `D0` suites, and keeps the no-widening
decision evidence-based.

## Intended outputs

- one `E1b` milestone scaffold;
- one lane-local export producing per-case component costs, suite-level bridge
  rows, history-bridge rows, and claim-impact wording;
- synchronized systems ledgers under the same mixed-gate interpretation.

## Scope lock

- current positive `D0` suites only;
- no frontend widening;
- no arbitrary-C or runtime-generalization claim;
- geometry asymptotics and end-to-end competitiveness remain separate.

## Acceptance

- the mixed systems gate is better explained, not widened;
- one bounded bottleneck decision is recorded from data rather than guesswork;
- `M7` remains a no-widening decision unless the new attribution clearly
  reverses the current gate.
